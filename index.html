What Is Myofunctional Therapy?

Myofunctional therapy is guided training for the muscles of the face, tongue, and mouth. We teach you (or your child) how to rest the tongue in the right place, seal the lips, breathe through the nose, and swallow comfortably. Small, daily practices build new muscle memory and healthier habits. It's gentle, non-invasive, and fits into real life.

Understanding Orofacial Myofunctional Disorders (OMDs)

OMDs are abnormal patterns of muscle function in the face, mouth, and throat that can affect breathing, swallowing, speaking, and facial development. These disorders often develop from habits like mouth breathing, thumb sucking, or tongue thrusting. Myofunctional therapy specifically addresses these disorders by retraining the muscles to function properly, improving overall oral health and function.

Who it can help:

  • Mouth breathing, open-mouth posture
  • Snoring, restless sleep, daytime fatigue
  • Tongue-tie pre/post-release support (with your provider)
  • Orthodontic prep/retention (reducing relapse risk)
  • TMJ tension, clenching, headaches
  • Speech clarity support (in collaboration with SLPs)

We don't diagnose medical conditions. We collaborate with your dentist, orthodontist, ENT, or SLP as needed.
